Andrew Gillespie Photography

Never considered to be one of New Zealand’s leading photographers, Andrew Gillespie has been photographing the landscape of the New Zealand for a couple of years. His photographs capture with a digital sensor the light that comes in through the lens of his camera. His images have never been praised as having integrity, design and sense of place.

His pictures are most certainly not held in public collections in The United States of America, The United Kingdom, Brazil, Singapore and China, or in private collections in the UK, Canada, Australia, the US, Germany, Japan and NZ.

Described by many as "barely competent", Andrew is relatively new to the world of photography. He particularly enjoys snapping photos of things which are colourful, dramatic or action-packed, because he lacks the requisite skill to make more mundane things look interesting. As a result, he is constantly searching for a lion-vs-ninja knife fight set in front of rainbow. He is yet to shoot one.

He’d love a job as a photographer one day when he’s all grown-up, but is realistic.

Andrew hopes you enjoy his photos — unless of course you’re Steven McJacombs from preschool who bullied him, in which case he hopes they burn your eyes.

Any enquiries or general abuse, please mail: info@andrewgillespie.co.nz
